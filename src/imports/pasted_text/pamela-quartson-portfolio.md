The Ask: Explore 3 Different Storytelling Approaches
Don't just layout the same content three ways. Explore three fundamentally different narratives for how to present this work. The three case studies are fixed, but how you frame them, prioritize them, and guide the viewer through them should be different.
Here are three possible frameworks, but treat them as inspiration, not instruction. Feel free to interpret, reframe, or invent a fourth approach if it feels right:
Approach One: Lead with what matters

What if the first thing a visitor sees isn't "about me" but "here's what I built and why it changed things"? Start with measurable outcomes. Let impact be the hook. People care that 2,100+ researchers now have better data. People care that someone saved their team 80% of their reporting time. Build from there.
Approach Two: Journey through systems

What if you organized by the type of problem solved, not by company or timeline? One section about building reliable pipelines. One about analytics that inform decisions. One about AI systems that learn from unstructured data. Let someone see: "Oh, they've done the kind of work we need." Show the progression of complexity or scope if it feels natural.
Approach Three: Problem first, then mastery

What if each project opened with the core problem in human terms, then revealed the technical sophistication underneath? "Decision makers were blind to their own data" (VROD's problem). "No one knew if their AI was actually working" (GraphRAG's problem). Let the technical depth unfold as you scroll, not as the opener. Make it memorable by starting with the friction.
